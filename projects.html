GIS and Cartography:

While I have dabbled in map making for several years, 2020 marked my first official steps into the world of GIS and cartography. I am currently enrolled in the Cartographic Specialist program offered by Fleming College and have enjoyed every minute of it. Some of the projects I have had the opportunity to work on include site suitability analyses, remote sensing image interpretation and analysis, and various cartographic mapping projects. Outside of assigned schoolwork I have enjoyed completing online tutorials by other cartographers.

Archaeological Work in Ireland:

One project that stands out for me during my time working the republic of Ireland was my participation on the N5 roadworks project with Archaeological Management Solutions. This linear road works project resulted in the excavation of numerous archaeological sites along the proposed impact alignment, including an Early Christian ring fort that I was fortunate to be a crew member on. While little remained visible prior to excavations, the removal of topsoil revealed a large circular ditch with numerous internal pit features and a stone lined souterrain feature. The Early Christian period in Ireland dates to between AD400 and AD1100. Some of the artefacts excavated included bone needles, bone weaving tools, stone spindle whorls, knife blades, and a silver semi-penannular triskele designed brooch.
